Our Story

A History of Growing with UK Business

Digital Marketing Professionals Ltd was incorporated on 15 October 2021 in London, with a clear mission: to make enterprise-grade IT consultancy and digital services accessible to small and medium-sized businesses across the UK.

From our registered office at 48 West Ham Lane, London E15, we began serving clients who needed practical, no-nonsense guidance on technology adoption, digital marketing, and business strategy — without the complexity or cost of large consulting firms.

Over the past three-plus years, we have expanded our capabilities across four core service areas — IT consultancy, management consulting, digital advertising, and software licensing — building a reputation for delivering real value to every client we serve.

Meet Our Director

Digital Marketing Professionals Ltd is led by an experienced IT professional with a strong track record in technology and business consulting.

Dr Charles Bowie Omorodion
Founder & Director — appointed 15 October 2021

Dr Charles Bowie Omorodion is a highly experienced IT professional with over a decade of expertise spanning information technology consultancy, business strategy, and digital transformation. A British national based in London, Dr Omorodion founded Digital Marketing Professionals Ltd in October 2021 with the vision of bridging the gap between enterprise-level technology capability and the practical needs of UK small and medium-sized businesses.

With a background that encompasses cloud solutions architecture, IT infrastructure management, software licensing strategy, and digital marketing, Dr Omorodion brings a uniquely holistic perspective to client engagements. His approach combines technical rigour with a commercial mindset — ensuring that every technology decision is grounded in measurable business value.

Under his leadership, Digital Marketing Professionals Ltd has grown into a trusted consultancy serving clients across multiple industries, helping them navigate the complexities of digital transformation, optimise their technology spend, and compete more effectively in an increasingly digital economy.
